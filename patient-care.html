Health Precautions

Everyday Care for Common Conditions

Simple, doctor-recommended precautions — in English and Tamil — to help you stay healthy and recover well.

Diabetic Foot Ulcer / Type 2 Diabetes

English
  • Keep the feet clean at all times.
  • Do not keep the legs hanging down for long; keep the affected foot elevated.
  • Avoid excessive walking.
  • Take diabetes medications regularly as prescribed.
  • Eat plenty of vegetables, greens and fibre-rich foods.

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)

English
  • Strongly reduce salt, pickles, dried fish, papad, chips and sweets.
  • Avoid foods high in sugar, ghee and oil; avoid deep-fried snacks.
  • Drink the recommended amount of water (about 1–1.5 L/day, including milk, coffee, juice).
  • Follow a kidney-friendly diet: low-salt, low-protein, restricted potassium and phosphorus.
  • Stop smoking, avoid alcohol; control blood pressure and blood sugar.
  • Exercise regularly, sleep well (~6 hours) and review kidney function every 3–4 months.

Before & After Surgery

Pre & Post-Operative Care Guide

Common questions answered for our surgical patients. Always follow your surgeon’s specific advice.

General Guidance for All Surgeries

  • There is no need to fear surgery — Dr. S. Sasiraj (MS, DNB) has performed 30,000+ surgeries since 2001.
  • The anaesthesiologist will advise which routine medicines (BP, sugar, etc.) to take or pause before surgery.
  • Heart medicines such as Aspirin and Clopilet must be stopped one week before surgery.
  • Thyroid medicines (Eltroxin / Thyronorm) — take with a little water at 5:00 AM on the day of surgery.
  • Bathe thoroughly with soap before coming in for surgery.
  • Post-op diet: avoid spicy/oily foods, butter, ghee, dalda and sweets; drink enough water; eat plenty of vegetables and fruits.

A discharge of clear fluid or blood mixed with pus from the surgical site can be normal; if there is excessive bleeding, contact our doctor. Avoid sitting on the floor or in a low position and do not lift heavy objects for one month. After one month you can freely resume all activities.

There is no need to be afraid. You can bathe after three days and may gently wash the surgical site with soap.

For those who do not yet have children, this surgery actually increases the chances of conceiving. Avoid spicy/oily foods, butter, ghee, dalda and sweets; increase water intake and eat plenty of vegetables and fruits.

If you have high blood pressure, diabetes or heart disease, medications may be paused only on the anaesthesiologist’s advice. All regular medications can be resumed after the surgery.

The chance of recurrence is extremely low. The wound takes about fifteen days to one month to heal. Continue sitz baths for at least fifteen days, and follow the recommended diet.

You can bathe once you return home. Avoid lifting weights and riding a bicycle or motorcycle for one month. Wear trousers, avoid bumpy roads and back seats, and do not do strenuous work for one month.

The pain and wound take about fifteen days to settle. After one month of rest you can sit on the floor and resume all daily activities. Regular medications can be taken after surgery. You may bathe one week after the surgery.

There is no need to be afraid. The wound heals in 15 days to a month and pain settles in about 15 days. You can bathe freely after returning home and should apply ointment to the site. Regular medications can be taken after surgery. Drink plenty of water and include turmeric, cumin, black pepper, cabbage, cauliflower, pomegranate and guava in your diet.

You can bathe freely after returning home. The wound heals in about two weeks; afterwards, learn the proper dressing technique. You can ride a bicycle/motorcycle after a month, depending on the wound. Discharge from the wound reduces gradually as it heals, and regular medications can be taken after surgery.

You may bathe after returning home, but keep the graft site dry. Do not change the dressing at home — come to the hospital to have it changed. Avoid spicy/oily foods and eat plenty of vegetables and fruits.

Keep the affected leg elevated and do not soak the wound in water. Dressing should be done daily or on alternate days as advised. Control blood sugar regularly, follow the diet, and use appropriate footwear to help healing.

Always keep the leg elevated when sitting or sleeping, and wear a crepe bandage while working during the day.

This guide is for general information only and does not replace your doctor’s advice. For any concern after surgery, contact us — there is no need to worry.
